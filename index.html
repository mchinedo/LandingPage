The Black@Brown Experience

    What is Black@Brown?

    Black@Brown is a term created by Black Students at Brown University to provide a space for Black Students navigating a predominately white institution. From being the title of GroupMe Chat names, to slogans on t-shirts, the term "Black@Brown" became very popular and is a huge part of the day to day lives of Black students at Brown.

    Throughout my time at Brown, I've gotten several requests from aspiring and incoming Black Brown University students about what it is really like being a Black Person at Brown University. Therefore, I decided to make this landing page highlighting the major events that the Black community looks forward to every year!

    There are numerous amounts of events and traditions in the Black community, and my hope is that one day, all interested students will get to experience them! In the meantime, I've listed 3 major events below to provide a quick glimpse into what it's like being "Black@Brown"

    I hope you enjoy!

    BSU Showcase

    The Annual BSU Pageant, now entitled Black Excellence Showcase, is an event to celebrate Black excellence, Black talent, and Black beauty. It is an important moment during which we dedicate ourselves to Black joy.

    Every year, the pageant is judged by a panel of professional members of our community and the previous year's pageant winners. The showcase winners win prizes!

    Click the button below to see videos and photos from this event!

    Black Appreciation Dinner (BAD)

    The Black Appreciation Dinner, also known as B.A.D, is an annual event hosted by the Black Heritage Series, which is a subgroup under the Brown Center for Students of Color whose goal is to create spaces on campus for identifying individuals. The Black Heritage Series collaborates with other Black organziations such as The League of Black Women, Black Students Association (BSU), and African Students Association (AfriSA) to put together the Black Appreciation Dinner

    The Black Appreciation Dinner is an event that aims to celebrate joy and excellence in the Black Community at Brown! This night is filled with performances, amazing food, faculty awards, senior appreciation, and more!

    BAD is always an event to look forward to!

    AfriSA Culture Show

    The African Students Association, also known as AfriSA, is a cultural organization that aims to provide spaces for students who identify as African or are interested in learning more about African Culture and traditions.

    The AfriSA Annual Culture Show is a chance for students to celebrate and witness different aspects of African Culture! This event is one of the biggest events held on campus! It’s a night filled with amazing food, speakers, community and performances from Brown dance groups as well as groups from schools such as MIT, Harvard, Yale, Columbia etc.

    The AfriSA Culture Show is definitely an event to see!
